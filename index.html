Introduction

My name is Marcus Diaz, and I’m a senior at the University of Rhode Island majoring in Computer Science. While most of my academic work focuses on programming and data systems, I took WRT 305 – a travel and food writing course – to explore a different side of storytelling. This class taught me how to slow down, reflect, and observe the world through the lens of food and culture.

Throughout the semester, I conducted field interviews, researched food-related issues, and experimented with different writing styles and genres. I learned to balance creativity with structure, and gained skills in crafting grant proposals, travel blogs, and reflective essays.

This site serves as my digital portfolio for WRT 305. You’ll find my completed works organized by category: a grant proposal to support community food literacy, a personal travel blog capturing a local culinary experience, and reflections that trace my growth as a writer. I invite you to explore each section using the navigation links above.

Thank you for visiting and engaging with my work!
