Travel Blog Research

Marcus Diaz

Genre Context

According to Rolf Potts, despite profound societal and technological changes, “the core task of travel writing—going slow, experiencing, listening, seeking nuance, reflecting—hasn’t changed much” over the years.

The travel blog genre refers to web-based journaling platforms where individuals go somewhere and share their experiences online through writing-intensive posts, photos, and videos. The genre offers insight, information, stories, and facts about a wide variety of destinations.

In this genre, point-of-view matters. A strong travel blog not only reports on what’s being experienced, but also tells readers about the “who” behind the experience. Readers should get to know the author, connect with their perspective, and want to follow their journey.

Audience Persona

Psychographics

  • Hobbies: Cooking traditional foods, visiting local farmers' markets, watching New England-based food shows, reading regional history, attending heritage events.
  • Values: Preserving Rhode Island culture, supporting local businesses, eating natural foods, family traditions, passing down culinary knowledge.
  • Political Preference: Moderate to progressive; supports environmental preservation, local agriculture, cultural education.
  • Religious Preference: Spiritually inclined or casually Christian/Catholic, not overtly religious.
  • Life Goals: Stay rooted in community, maintain Rhode Island identity, live meaningfully, retire in-state, be a “keeper of tradition.”
  • Fears: Losing cultural heritage, decline of local businesses, tech overwhelm, loss of RI’s food culture.

Demographics

  • Age: 45–65
  • Gender: Female (could also be male or couple)
  • Sexuality: Heterosexual
  • Profession: Teacher, librarian, small business owner, retired artisan
  • Income Level: $40,000–$80,000/year
  • Dis/abilities: May include mild arthritis, dietary sensitivities
  • Location: South County, Rhode Island (Wakefield, West Kingston, Usquepaugh)

Audience Research Questions

1) What problem or desire will your project help this person solve/satisfy?

This blog will help satisfy their desire to remain connected to Rhode Island heritage and culinary traditions. It will offer insight into the history behind Johnnycakes and encourage pride in preserving local foodways. It may also inspire a visit to Kenyon’s Grist Mill and support a historic local business.

2) How should this person be changed after reading/watching/experiencing your project?

After reading the blog, the audience should feel more informed and emotionally connected to the history of Johnnycakes, inspired to cook them at home with authentic ingredients, and motivated to continue preserving Rhode Island’s food heritage. Ideally, they’ll feel a deeper sense of identity as part of an ongoing cultural story.
